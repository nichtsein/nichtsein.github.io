The Decline of the Church

發表於 2020-08-30 分類於 The Humanistic Tradition

   The two relative events dwarfed the prestige and authority of the church, the Avignon Papacy and the Great Schism. The Avignon Papacy means a relocation of the papacy from Rome to a city of Avignon. This relocation is a response to the rise of nation-states in secular influence, especially to political pressure from the French king Philip IV. The construction of a luxurious court of the Avignon popes contributed to the increasing need for church revenue. The sequence of that led simony which is a corruption that sold church offices, to levy more taxes upon clergymen, and to step up the sale of indulgences. These unmoral efforts at the range of finance and politics damaged the reputation of the church.

Samurai

發表於 2020-08-24 分類於 The Humanistic Tradition

Samurai, skilled warriors in ancient Japan, belonged to a specific rank in society, held land in return for local landlords. Land as a property reflects that the transmission of a power or a right, that had been seen in traditional Japan. By means of lands and the relationship of power generated by lands, samurai with the arts of archery and swordsmanship devoted their allegiance for clans and protected them from riots. The specific rank sustained the stability of society during the peaceful period, but at the time of rebellion they played a key role in rebelling social order.

Chinese Landscape Painting

發表於 2020-08-22 分類於 The Humanistic Tradition

At the time of the Tang Era, landscape painting became the popular genre of Chinese art. The literati viewed landscape paintings as wordless poems, and poems as formless paintings,such metaphors reflect the close relationship between painting and poetry in Chinese art. Unlike the Christian paintings, the landscape paintings prefer to evoke the emotion rather than provide a objective description of reality. And landscape paintings are more secular less relative to religion. The specific nature of Chinese landscape painting is that human occupations seem mundane and incidental. So what display in front of your eyes are mountains, mist, rivers, waterfalls, flying birds, and a variety of trees in landscape paintings.
