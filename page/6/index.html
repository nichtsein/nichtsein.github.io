The Effects of the Black Death
15-09-2020 Guanghua Tower

  In the first place, we see psychological effects of the Black Death. Those who saved their lives after this plague tried to fathom its meaning, some viewed it as a manifestation of something, especially of God’s displeasure. Plenty of flagellants whipped their bodies in frenzies of self-mortification as a way of atonement. Group of clergy stopped their profligacy and moral laxity. Common people had a happy lifestyle with the belief of “eat, drink, and be merry”. And those who tried to find why god made this to humankind.

  Secondly, the feeling of fear for plague set forth in visual arts and poetry. the most popular plague-related motif was the “Dance of Death”, it picture that a skeleton or corpse lead his victims to grave. In addition, the Death have been objectified as a “the Great Equalizer”, all of humankind are succumb to it whatever their race, social status, wealth, but just one point that they are vulnerable human. The plague spurred the rise of sense of equality.
