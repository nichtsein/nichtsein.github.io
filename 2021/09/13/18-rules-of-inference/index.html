Implication (Impl)

When you know that the statement x → y is true, you know that either x is false (that is, $\neg x$ is true) or that y is true. In other words, you know that the statement $\neg x \vee y$ is true.

Distribution (Dist)

I have a pet and it’s either a cat or a dog. $x \wedge ( y \vee z)$

Either I have a pet and it’s a cat or I have a pet and it’s a dog. $(x \wedge y) \vee( x \wedge z)$

I have to take either organic chemistry or both botany and zoology. $x \vee ( y \wedge z)$

I have to take either organic chemistry or botany, and I also have to take either organic chemistry or zoology. $(x \vee y) \wedge( x \vee z)$

DeMorgan’s Theorem (DeM)

 

It’s not true that I’m both rich and famous. $\neg (x\wedge y)$

Either I’m not rich or I’m not famous. $\neg x \vee \neg y$

Jack is neither a doctor nor a lawyer. $\neg (x\vee y) $

Jack isn’t a doctor and he isn’t a lawyer. $\neg x \wedge \neg y$
